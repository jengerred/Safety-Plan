Safety Plan

Author: Jennifer Gerred

The following are suggestions of what to include or think about in your safety plan to keep you and your children safe from domestic violence:


  • What could you use as a CODE WORD with your children/family/friends so they can call for help?

  • During times when you are home alone, who can stay with you if you don’t want to be alone?

  • If you’re not comfortable being at home where else could you go to stay safe?

  • If you need to leave your home in an emergency, where is a safe place you could go?

  • If you need to leave your house quickly, which items would you need to take with you?

  • If you need to leave immediately, whom can you call to pick you up or meet you?

  • What important people and phone numbers should you have accessible to your children and yourself?

  • Who would you leave copies of important documents, an extra set of car and/or house keys, money, and extra clothes with?

  • If you have to flee right away, where can you go and who can you call?

  • Do your children know how to call 911?

  • Is there anyone you can tell about the past violence and request they call the police if they hear suspicious noises coming from my home?

  • Practice how to get out of your home safely. What doors, windows or exits can be used in an emergency?

  • Who could watch your children if you need childcare in an emergency?

    • What other things can you do to increase your safety?
